India is now the second most populous country in the world. The resources to support every single person on the Earth, are limited and hence not everyone is fortunate enough to be blessed with even the basic amenities required in life. We are all by now aware that some 1.77 million Indians are still homeless. It is very surprising to note that an analysis issued between March 9 and May 3, 2020, shows that 16 states with 40% of the countryâ€™s homeless make no official mention of them at all. Only Delhi, Maharashtra and Kerala talk about regular health checks and safety provisions for the homeless. Homeless people on the street are prone to trafficking, drug abuse and other crimes. A large section of this population comprises of women and children. They take shelter under bridges, on footpath, and under very vulnerable conditions. They are in dire need of help and there exist many organisations and people that wish to provide them the help they would need.
But there is no single clean pathway to monitor the exact living conditions and place of their stay, so that relief materials and proper health support can reach these homeless people. This is the utmost need of the hour. If there no genuine information about these section of people, then no one can lend them a helping hand. If there is enough availability of information about the people then one can come up with the correct amount of help. That would be very much effective and time saving.
So, we the " Turning Crypt " team have come up with a solution that can monitor as well as provide the real time and completely authentic information to the NGOs and GOs and all the people that might want to help, the right information regarding the people so that they can reach out with help and provide the basic amenities to make their conditions better.
We live in a country which is for the people, of the people and by the people so it is one of our prime duties to come up together as a team to atleast try and help to uplift these people in a right way. Then only we can make our country a better place to live in.
